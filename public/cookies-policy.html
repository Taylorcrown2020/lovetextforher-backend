1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

1.1 Types of Cookies by Duration

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser. These are essential for basic website functionality.
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them. These remember your preferences and settings across visits.

1.2 Types of Cookies by Origin

  • First-Party Cookies: Set by LoveTextForHer directly. We have full control over these cookies.
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by external services we use (e.g., analytics providers, payment processors). These are governed by the third party's privacy policy.

2. How We Use Cookies

LoveTextForHer uses cookies for the following purposes:

2.1 Essential Cookies (Required)

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. You cannot opt out of these cookies.

  • Authentication: Keep you logged in to your account
  • Security: Protect against fraud and unauthorized access
  • Session Management: Maintain your session as you navigate the site
  • Load Balancing: Distribute traffic across our servers

2.2 Functional Cookies (Optional)

These cookies enhance functionality and personalization but are not essential.

  • Preferences: Remember your settings (language, timezone, theme)
  • Personalization: Customize content based on your usage
  • Form Data: Remember information you've entered in forms

2.3 Analytics Cookies (Optional)

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website.

  • Usage Tracking: Pages visited, time spent, navigation patterns
  • Performance Monitoring: Load times, errors, technical issues
  • A/B Testing: Compare different versions of features
  • Conversion Tracking: Measure effectiveness of marketing campaigns

2.4 Marketing Cookies (Optional)

These cookies track your activity across websites to deliver targeted advertising.

  • Retargeting: Show relevant ads on other websites
  • Social Media: Enable sharing and track social media interactions
  • Campaign Attribution: Measure which marketing channels are effective

5. How to Manage Cookies

You have several options to control and manage cookies:

5.1 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to:

  • View what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block all cookies from being set
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

How to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.2 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit LoveTextForHer, you'll see a cookie consent banner allowing you to:

  • Accept All: Allow all cookies including analytics and marketing
  • Decline: Block optional cookies (essential cookies still enabled)
  • Customize: Choose which categories of cookies to allow

5.3 Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of specific tracking:

  • Google Analytics: Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Advertising Cookies: Digital Advertising Alliance
  • Do Not Track: Enable "Do Not Track" in your browser settings

5.4 Impact of Disabling Cookies

Important: If you disable cookies, some features of LoveTextForHer may not work properly, including:

  • Staying logged in to your account
  • Saving your preferences and settings
  • Accessing certain features that require authentication
  • Personalized content and recommendations

6. Other Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use other tracking technologies:

6.1 Web Beacons (Pixels)

Small transparent images embedded in web pages or emails to track:

  • Whether you've opened an email
  • Which links you've clicked
  • Page view statistics

6.2 Local Storage

HTML5 local storage allows websites to store data locally in your browser:

  • Larger storage capacity than cookies
  • Does not expire automatically
  • Used for caching and offline functionality

6.3 Session Storage

Similar to local storage but data is cleared when the browser tab is closed.
